About Me

I'm from Algeria, a graduate with a biotechnology Engineering degree. Currently doing my PhD in Biomedical Engineering at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China.

Research Interests

Cancer

My Interest in cancer diagnosis and therapy focuses on refining early detection methods, exploring novel biomarkers and imaging technologies. Additionally, I'm deeply invested in advancing therapeutic approaches, especially in immunotherapy and targeted treatments, and pinpointing specific molecular pathways in cancer cells, in order to develop more effective and personalized treatments.

Microfluidics

My research passion in microfluidic devices is driven by their transformative potential in revolutionizing various fields, particularly in healthcare and biotechnology. These intricate systems, manipulating tiny amounts of fluids, offer a platform for innovation that transcends traditional laboratory techniques. These devices pave the way for rapid diagnostics, cost-effective analyses, and Personalized treatments.

Biosensors

I'm interested in these miniature devices, because of their capability of detecting specific biological molecules with incredible precision, which offer a gateway to a multitude of applications, from disease diagnosis, environmental monitoring, to enabling real-time point-of-care analyses.
